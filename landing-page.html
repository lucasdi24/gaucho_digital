The Rio Manso Experience

Where big fish live
in wild water

The Rio Manso carves through temperate rainforest and open steppe at the foot of the Andes — one of the last places on earth where wild brown and rainbow trout reach true trophy size in completely natural conditions.

Our season runs November through April — perfect timing for North American anglers looking to extend their season south of the equator.

  • ✓ Wild brown & rainbow trout in completely natural conditions
  • ✓ Dry fly, nymph & streamer — drift, wade, or sight-cast
  • ✓ All bilingual guides, 1:1 angler-to-guide ratio
  • ✓ The Dragonfly Hatch (Dec–Jan): trophy fish rising to dry flies
  • ✓ 55°F–75°F daytime temperatures throughout the season
  • ✓ On-site fly shop, sauna, spa, and beyond-angling activities

A Rare Natural Phenomenon

The Dragonfly Hatch

Each year, aquatic insects emerge from Patagonia's rivers in a concentrated burst that triggers explosive surface-feeding activity among trophy trout. It is one of fly fishing's most spectacular natural events — a brief, powerful window when the rivers come alive with rising fish of extraordinary size.
